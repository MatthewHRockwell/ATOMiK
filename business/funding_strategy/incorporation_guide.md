# CA LLC to DE C-Corp Conversion Guide

## Why Convert?

Most funding programmes (SBIR, YC SAFEs, VC rounds) require a Delaware C-Corp.
A statutory conversion preserves the existing EIN and contracts.

## 10-Step Process

### 1. Choose Conversion Service

| Option | Cost | Timeline |
|--------|------|----------|
| SeedLegals (automated) | ~$1,499 | 2-3 weeks |
| Stripe Atlas | ~$500 | 1-2 weeks |
| Attorney | $3,000-10,000 | 3-6 weeks |

### 2. Draft Plan of Conversion

Legal document describing:
- Current entity (CA LLC)
- Target entity (DE C-Corp)
- Conversion terms (membership → stock)

### 3. Member Approval

- Sole member: written consent is sufficient
- No vote required with single member

### 4. File with California Secretary of State

- Form SI-LLC (annual) must be current
- Certificate of Conversion filing
- Filing fee: ~$100

### 5. File with Delaware Secretary of State

- Certificate of Incorporation
- Certificate of Conversion
- Filing fee: ~$200-400

### 6. Post-Conversion Documents

- Bylaws
- Stock purchase agreement
- 83(b) election (file with IRS within 30 days)
- Board resolutions

### 7. IP Assignment

- Execute Founder → Company IP assignment
- Template: `business/data_room/02_legal/ip_assignment_template.md`

### 8. EIN Update

- EIN is preserved through statutory conversion
- Update business name with IRS if legal name changes
- Form 8822-B

### 9. Business Licence & Vendor Updates

- Update business licences with new entity type
- Notify bank, vendors, insurance

### 10. Delaware Registered Agent

Required for all Delaware entities:
- Annual cost: $50-300
- Service providers: SeedLegals, Northwest, Incfile

## Timeline

Total estimated: 2-4 weeks (automated service) to 6-8 weeks (attorney)
